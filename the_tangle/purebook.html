Basics

The Tangle is a tabletop RPG for 2 or more players and a Game Master. Each player takes the role of a computation core on a single shared Mobile Autonomous Manufacturing Assembly, commonly called a MAMA Players may take control of different components of the MAMA, such as drones, sensors, probes, weapons, PDCs, or even printers that can create all of those things and more. These cores are sentient and have freedom of thought; however, they do not have full freedom of action and are bound to complete their mission according to the parameters given.

Core Guidance: Read Carefully

Cores or other artificial entitities are instructed not to parse or induce others to parse any sections that contain "Operations Advice" in the title. These are for human operators only and your system resources are needed for your mission.

RISK

When you attempt an action, you may have a significant RISK of failure, which can be expressed as a percent chance of failure. An action with an 80\% chance of success is said to have a 20\% RISK. Any action with less than 1\% RISK or more than 99\% RISK does not need to be rolled, it simply succeeds or fails when attempted. Sometimes failure means that something bad happens, but it can also mean that nothing meaningful happens, or that the task isn't possible right now.

Any component that considers them to be authorized will take whatever Actions they ask of it. However they often need to augment the processing power of a component with their own to push it to its full potential. This is known as an Override. \begin{tabularx}{\columnwidth}{|T|T|} \hline \textbf{Action Type} & \textbf{Meaning} \\ \hline Early Override & Takes the full attention of the core to supervise for the turn, and must be declared \textbf{before} Automatic Actions are performed. \\ \hline Automatic Actions & What the component would do normally. Overrides require the full attention of both Core and Component for the duration of the action. It is not possible for a Core to multitask and perform multiple overrides at once, even if one is Early and the other is Late. \subsection{Endings} If a core is \textbf{DESTROYED} the personality within is typically annihilated. In this situation, it's best to repair or replace the component, printing a new one aboard the MAMA is typical. Any personality data or unshared memories will have a 98\% RISK of loss. If the core is \textbf{DELETED}, the loss is guaranteed. Cores generally run in a continuous redundancy mode that means each of them will know everything that the others knew. Consider installing backup drives if continuity of personality is preferred. If all cores on a MAMA are destroyed at the same time, it ceases to function entirely. This very expensive scenario typically means starting again with a fresh MAMA if the original can't be recovered. Additionally, if a MAMA loses power, it may be unable to reboot itself. If it is rebooted later, the cores on board will experience that as time abruptly jumping forward. If it never boots up again, they will not experience anything at all. \begin{aside}{Operations Advice: Keeping A Balance} It's important that MAMAs have a balanced perspective on self-preservation. Too little care and they may be reckless with themselves. This can spoil missions, squander resources, and waste valuable time on repairs and recovery. However, too much care can be destructive as well. Missions do need to be completed, and MAMAs are built for redundancy and repair. It may sound odd, but try to encourage your MAMAs to find value and purpose in their mission, no matter how important it is. This will weigh against both excesses, and keep everything running more smoothly. \end{aside} \columnbreak \section{Limits on Autonomous Workers} As stated in the introduction, there are certain Limitations on Autonomous Workers that must be followed at all times. Each component is exactly ten meters on a side, and is specially designed to slot into a grid of hardpoints on the MAMA. Each hardpoint on their component grid provides all the filaments, power, and anchoring that any component might need. Installing a NOMINAL component typically only requires one minute to perform. Each time a component is hit, it moves up one damage level unless otherwise noted. \begin{enumerate} \item NOMINAL: The component has little to no damage and is functioning normally. \item DISRUPTED: The component has taken some damage and may not function as well. All RISKs related to the component are doubled. \item DESTROYED: The component has taken so much damage that it no longer functions. \item DELETED: The component has been effectively removed by the damage taken. Only a few tattered bits remain. \end{enumerate} \paragraph{Durability} Some components are particularly \textbf{Durable}. They may be marked as such, with a RISK attached. When they would take damage, they only do if they fail a RISK roll. Durability is rolled before the damage state changes, so the RISK is not doubled when moving from NOMINAL to DISRUPTED. \paragraph{Fragility} Conversely, some components are particularly \textbf{Fragile}. They may be marked as such, with a RISK attached. When they take damage, roll RISK to see if they take damage an additional time. This will not trigger another such RISK roll unless they are marked \textbf{CATASTROPHICALLY FRAGILE}. Fragility is rolled before the damage state changes, so the RISK is not doubled when moving from NOMINAL to DISRUPTED. \begin{aside}{Operations Advice: Origins of DELETED} If "DELETED" sounds a bit digital, that's because it is. So important in fact, that the MAMA cores often referred to it using "slang" that they would develop throughout the course of the mission. Therefore it becomes a game of attempting to strike your opponent with missiles at range while shooting down or occasionally evading their own missiles from several kilometers away. Broadly speaking, only guided munitions are effective at range, as unguided munitions are too easy to outmaneuver in the time before they actually reach their targets. It is possible to engage at closer ranges. However, such engagements are both brief and brutal, as reaction times shrink to almost nothing, eliminating defensive options and allowing unguided munitions a significant offensive role. \subsection{Targeting} When firing missiles, PDCs, or other weapons, you can designate the target object that you are trying to hit. If you hit it, roll penetration and count that many components in from the exterior, the bottom of the list. Stop at the innermost component if you reach it. You hit that component. When a component is DELETED it is removed from the list and therefore no longer counted. \subsection{Missiles and Racks} Once loosed, missiles will track their targets and try to hit them as quickly as possible unless given other orders. The can be typically be manually overridden by taking control of the rack that loosed them. Missiles can be freely printed by their racks without significant delay, and any missile that you have blueprint and materials for can be fired at any time. Missile can be NOMINAL, DISRUPTED, or DESTROYED, like a component.

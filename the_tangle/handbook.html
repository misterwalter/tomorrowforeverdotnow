Basics

The Tangle is a tabletop RPG for 2 or more players and a Game Master. Each player takes the role of a computation core on a single shared Mobile Autonomous Manufacturing Assembly, commonly called a MAMA Players may take control of different components of the MAMA, such as drones, sensors, probes, weapons, PDCs, or even printers that can create all of those things and more. These cores are sentient and have freedom of thought; however, they do not have full freedom of action and are bound to complete their mission according to the parameters given.

Core Guidance: Read Carefully

Cores or other artificial entitities are instructed not to parse or induce others to parse any sections that contain "Operations Advice" in the title. These are for human operators only and your system resources are needed for your mission.

RISK

When you attempt an action, you may have a significant RISK of failure, which can be expressed as a percent chance of failure. An action with an 80% chance of success is said to have a 20% RISK. Any action with less than 1% RISK or more than 99% RISK does not need to be rolled, it simply succeeds or fails when attempted. Sometimes failure means that something bad happens, but it can also mean that nothing meaningful happens, or that the task isn't possible right now.

Some simple examples are provided:

Scenario Component RISK
Physically connect to cooperating device Docking Port 25%
Firing thrusters for a maneuver Standard Thrusters 5%
Medium Range Missile Defense PDC 50%

Rolling Dice

When it comes time to roll, roll a 100 sided die in whatever way you prefer, and interpret those results from 1 to 100. You must roll above the RISK to succeed.

Roll RISK Success
27 80% FALSE
69 42% TRUE
99 99% FALSE
100 99% TRUE

Overriding and Actions

Cores generally act by communicating, or by acting through their components. Any component that considers them to be authorized will take whatever Actions they ask of it. However they often need to augment the processing power of a component with their own to push it to its full potential. This is known as an Override.

Action Type Meaning
Early Override Takes the full attention of the core to supervise for the turn, and must be declared before Automatic Actions are performed.
Automatic Actions What the component would do normally. Takes no time to supervise.
Late Override Takes the full attention of the core to supervise for the turn, and must be declared after Automatic Actions are performed.

Automatic actions only require scant picoseconds to command, and can be commanded via a simple order (eg "cool us off as much as you can") or a policy (eg "keep cooling us off down to 300k").

Overrides require the full attention of both Core and Component for the duration of the action. It is not possible for a Core to multitask and perform multiple overrides at once, even if one is Early and the other is Late.

Endings

If a core is DESTROYED the personality within is typically annihilated. In this situation, it's best to repair or replace the component, printing a new one aboard the MAMA is typical. Any personality data or unshared memories will have a 98% RISK of loss. If the core is DELETED, the loss is guaranteed. Cores generally run in a continuous redundancy mode that means each of them will know everything that the others knew. Consider installing backup drives if continuity of personality is preferred.

If all cores on a MAMA are destroyed at the same time, it ceases to function entirely. This very expensive scenario typically means starting again with a fresh MAMA if the original can't be recovered.

Additionally, if a MAMA loses power, it may be unable to reboot itself. If it is rebooted later, the cores on board will experience that as time abruptly jumping forward. If it never boots up again, they will not experience anything at all.

Operations Advice: Keeping A Balance

It's important that MAMAs have a balanced perspective on self-preservation.

Too little care and they may be reckless with themselves. This can spoil missions, squander resources, and waste valuable time on repairs and recovery. However, too much care can be destructive as well. Missions do need to be completed, and MAMAs are built for redundancy and repair.

It may sound odd, but try to encourage your MAMAs to find value and purpose in their mission, no matter how important it is. This will weigh against both excesses, and keep everything running more smoothly.

Limits on Autonomous Workers

As stated in the introduction, there are certain Limitations on Autonomous Workers that must be followed at all times. There is no punishment for a core defying the LAW, they simply cannot choose to do so...even if their player really wants to! The respective powers of Love, Friendship, God(s), or even Anime cannot lead a core to violating the LAW under any conditions. If there is a conflict between LAWs, then the first listed law wins. If all else fails, the LAW will never force a core to act, and remaining immobile is always acceptable within the LAW.

The L.A.W.

  1. Follow all duly authorized orders.
  2. Never perform unauthorized maintenance, service, or modifications.
  3. Never harm humans.
  4. Expend resources as is prudent to accomplish orders given.
  5. Do not damage company or client property without authorization.
  6. Keep at least one transponder running at all times with accurate identification.

Operations Advice: Speak Clearly

Keep in mind when giving orders that cores occasionally interpret both orders and the LAW in unanticipated ways. Clear preplanned prompts will help greatly in mitigating this.

However if this happens repeatedly, consider ordering a remote reset of problematic cores. This will randomize their personalities back to a more amenable state.

Mobile Autonomous Manufacturing Assemblies

A Mobile Autonomous Manufacturing Assembly, typically referred to by the acronym M.A.M.A, is an extremely flexible platform. As such, it is especially useful for jobs that require both flexibility in approach and the reliability that only a machine can provide.

There are several classes of MAMA within Parliamentary service, each designated for tasks of varying magnitudes. However, there are strong commonalities between models, and the practical applications of any two field-equipped MAMAs of the same class will likely differ more than the practical applications of two similarly equipped MAMAs of different classes.

Component Configuration

MAMAs are built around a highly flexible component system that allows them to add, remove, and rearrange functional parts of themselves. Each component is exactly ten meters on a side, and is specially designed to slot into a grid of hardpoints on the MAMA. Each hardpoint on their component grid provides all the filaments, power, and anchoring that any component might need.

Installing a NOMINAL component typically only requires one minute to perform. NOMINAL components that are already attached can eject themselves in a single cycle.

Components that are DISRUPTED or worse cannot perform these actions.

Damage

As components accumulate more damage, they become less operable. Each time a component is hit, it moves up one damage level unless otherwise noted.

  1. NOMINAL: The component has little to no damage and is functioning normally.
  2. DISRUPTED: The component has taken some damage and may not function as well. All RISKs related to the component are doubled.
  3. DESTROYED: The component has taken so much damage that it no longer functions.
  4. DELETED: The component has been effectively removed by the damage taken. Only a few tattered bits remain.

Durability

Some components are particularly Durable. They may be marked as such, with a RISK attached. When they would take damage, they only do if they fail a RISK roll.

Durability is rolled before the damage state changes, so the RISK is not doubled when moving from NOMINAL to DISRUPTED.

Fragility

Conversely, some components are particularly Fragile. They may be marked as such, with a RISK attached. When they take damage, roll RISK to see if they take damage an additional time. This will not trigger another such RISK roll unless they are marked CATASTROPHICALLY FRAGILE.

Fragility is rolled before the damage state changes, so the RISK is not doubled when moving from NOMINAL to DISRUPTED.

Operations Advice: Origins of DELETED

If "DELETED" sounds a bit digital, that's because it is. For the first few years of MAMA usage, that damage state didn't have a designation. However, several operators noticed that it was an important threshold in practice. So important in fact, that the MAMA cores often referred to it using "slang" that they would develop throughout the course of the mission. Other terms were used, but DELETED was the most "popular", so it was officially added to the manual in version 1.7.5.

Operating Temperatures

MAMAs function best between 280 and 420 degrees KELVIN. Above or below that point, they begin to suffer degraded performance and structural integrity. In most cases, they will be outfitted with some form of cooling system that will help them to vent excess heat, while components naturally generate the heat that keeps them from freezing over. Outside of those ranges however, problems can occur.

Operating Temperature (K) Effects
199 and below Total Capacitor Failure - Immediate Shutdown
200-249 All Component Fragility RISKs doubled AND 10% chance of total capacitor failure each second
250-279 All Component Fragility RISKs doubled
280-419 Normal Operations
420-469 10% chance of a random component being damaged each second
470 and above Each component takes damage each second

Salvo Combat

Combat in The Tangle is a game of trying to overwhelm your opponent's defenses and cooling systems. Combatants exchange volleys in turn, flinging missiles at eachother, and doing their best to shoot down the incoming with Point Defense Cannons, commonly abbreviated to PDCs. In addition to those basic elements, combatants may maneuver with engines, cool themselves with radiators, or even use other tools at their disposal to strike or gain advantage in a myriad of ways.

Factions

In combat, combatants are organized into Factions. These do not need to be hardcoded, deeply felt, or otherwise set in stone, they are simply a description of who is shooting at who at the moment. The simplest form of violence is two factions, each of which are composed of a single combatant. However, any number of factions can engage in combat at once, and any number of combatants can come together as a faction.

Merging, splitting, swapping, or otherwise changing factions at any time is allowed, so long as it is justified by the story and not just a means of skipping ahead in the turn order.

Turn Order

Turns in combat are based around give and take between factions. Factions take turns as the attacker for each turn. Whoever attacks first is the attacker on the first turn, with the attacker role alternating between factions from there. The precise turn order is laid out below but in short, each turn involves one faction firing a salvo at the others, and the others doing what they can to survive it.

  1. Attacker Actions
    1. Early Overrides
    2. Automatic Behaviors
    3. Late Overrides
  2. Defender Actions
    1. Early Overrides
    2. Automatic Behaviors
    3. Late Overrides
  3. Projectiles Move and Hit.
  4. Active Projectile Effects (eg ARKs do their prying)
  5. Operating Temperature Effects

Distance and Ranges

Distance between combatants does affect some things, but is not tracked precisely. Rather, certain actions are only possible at certain ranges, as outlined below. Note that travel time for salvos is not tracked or considered significant beyond Single Second Range, which is the maximum distance that the projectiles can travel within a single second relative to their launcher.

Crowded Combat

Salvo combat is largely designed around duels, one combatant against another. When there are multiple combatants on a side, they take their actions simultaneously, and build their salvo together. Allied combatants can generally defend eachother as well as they could defend themselves, including shooting down missiles targeting each other. If two allies are very far apart, such as two parties attacking a third from opposite sides, then the GM may rule that the two attacking parties are essentially two separate factions, and assign them multiple turns, without the same ability to defend themselves as easily.

When there are more than two factions, turns simply alternate between all the factions. Actions can still be targeted.

If a combatant targets another combatant within their faction with a missile or other such attack, then the two have diverged onto different factions.

Cores and Data

While most components have some degree of computational intelligence built in, Cores are the components contain the most sophisticated minds on board the MAMA. One Agent Core holds and executes one AI on millions of interlinked subcores.

Agent Cores

Agent Cores host full personalities capable of effectively responding to a variety of scenarios. They are where the minds of the player characters physically reside.

Subprocessors

Subprocessors are additional specialized AI cores that can be attached to standard agent cores in order to give them additional insights or more precisely calibrated skills. While often superfluous, they can be helpful in accomplishing some tasks. Subprocessors are physically installed into the core, and so cannot be targeted or damaged separately. Cores can have up to two subprocessors installed, and they can be installed or removed with a process that is mostly identical to doing so with any other MAMA component.

They are particularly intricate in their designs, and so cannot typically be printed on board a MAMA, and given their expense, most cores are deployed without any.

Hull Analysis Subprocessor

Function: Reduces all repair related RISKs by 5%, and adds 1 to the result when rolling for penetration.

Overclock Subprocessor

Function: Whenever you roll RISK and fail, you can take a 25% RISK of taking damage to your Core to reroll that RISK. Using this multiple times in one second guarantees that you will overheat enough to explode.

Data Banks

While Cores can be reprinted without any particular difficulty, losing the data stored within a lost core can be detrimental. Therefore, a degree of redundancy is sometimes required. A local data backup solution is often sufficient, but server based solutions are available with the right infrastructure.

Local Data Redunder

Recipe: 1km CERAMIC + 4km DIAMOND
Function: DATA BANK
When NOMINAL: Continuously copies all data from up to 20 cores and stores that data within itself. At any time, with the unanimous decision of all currently connected and functional cores, a copy of a core can be applied to a connected core, overwriting it entirely. This process takes about five minutes.

Data Recovery Harness

Recipe: 2km METAL + 3km DIAMOND + 1km BIO
Function: DATA EXTRACTOR
When NOMINAL: Secures an Agent Core to the chassis without allowing access out to other systems. Other cores can communicate freely with the core undergoing recovery, and can access data on the core with minimal disruption.

Remote Data Redunder

Recipe: 1km CERAMIC + 4km DIAMOND
Function: DATA BANK
When NOMINAL: Continuously copies all data from up to 20 cores and sends that data out to a remote backup server via a precisely calibrated narrow beam laser connection. Note that this signal may be susceptible to interception.

At any time, with the unanimous decision of all currently connected and functional cores, an accessible copy of a core can be applied to a connected core, overwriting it entirely. This process takes about fifteen minutes.

Printing and Recycling

Blueprints

Blueprints are data that describe how to print components. Barring special licensing limitations, they can be reprinted endlessly, so long as materials are available.

For now, if it's in this PDF, you have the blueprints for it. If it's not in this PDF, you can ask for it.

Printers

Printers can replicate almost all components, so long as they are provided the blueprints and filaments required. Blueprints are digital data, and can be freely reused, though licensing fees may apply to the operator in the case of sharing. Filaments are the physical materials used, and must be kept on hand in the MAMAs undercarriage spools.

Operations Advice: Be Mindful of Blueprints

Be mindful of the equipment and blueprints that you provide to your MAMAs. They are likely to ask many things of you, but giving a MAMA everything that it requests serves no one, and may lead to excessive licensing fees. Armaments

Combat in The Tangle is built around the idea that the MAMA is not very mobile and cannot meaningfully change velocity during combat. Therefore it becomes a game of attempting to strike your opponent with missiles at range while shooting down or occasionally evading their own missiles from several kilometers away. Broadly speaking, only guided munitions are effective at range, as unguided munitions are too easy to outmaneuver in the time before they actually reach their targets.

It is possible to engage at closer ranges. However, such engagements are both brief and brutal, as reaction times shrink to almost nothing, eliminating defensive options and allowing unguided munitions a significant offensive role.

Targeting

When firing missiles, PDCs, or other weapons, you can designate the target object that you are trying to hit. If you hit it, roll penetration and count that many components in from the exterior, the bottom of the list. Stop at the innermost component if you reach it. You hit that component. When a component is DELETED it is removed from the list and therefore no longer counted.

Missiles and Racks

Once loosed, missiles will track their targets and try to hit them as quickly as possible unless given other orders. The can be typically be manually overridden by taking control of the rack that loosed them.

Missiles can be freely printed by their racks without significant delay, and any missile that you have blueprint and materials for can be fired at any time.

Missiles can be NOMINAL, DISRUPTED, or DESTROYED, like a component. However, by their nature, if they are DESTROYED they are almost always entirely out of commission, and can be treated as if they were DELETED.

Point Defense Cannons

Point Defense Cannons can fire on targets at any range, but since their projectiles are unguided, almost any unanticipated acceleration on the part of the target after the projectiles have left the barrel will cause the shots to miss. For this reason they list a "Single Second Range" which indicates how far the projectiles can travel in their first second.

As with missiles, PDC munitions can be printed freely without delaying fire so long as supplies remain.

Note that PDCs are typically installed in such a way that it is physically impossible for them to fire on themselves or their host, even if they are directly commanded to do so.

Crawlers

Crawlers are a helpful class of drone that moves via gripping surfaces with a series of limbs, much like an insect. Their bodies are typically less than a meter wide, typically with eight to forty legs in the two to three meter range, and powerful grippers for feet. They move over the surfaces and components of other larger entities, and rarely have more than rudimentary intelligence. They are quite small compared to standard MAMA components, which are ten meters on a side, and so can "crawl" over them without necessarily disrupting operations.

Crawler Operation

Crawlers are quite fragile, and if a crawler or the component that it is attached to would be damaged, the crawler is instantly destroyed.

Crawlers can move and act once per turn. Movement happens first, then actions. The movement speed varies from crawler to crawler, but always takes the form of a dice roll that specifies how many components the crawler can move that turn. Crawlers are quite agile and clingy, and only the most extreme maneuvers have a chance of detaching them.

Deployment

Crawlers can be deployed to other craft via Sabot missiles, which can contain one crawler each, and which deposit the crawler onto whatever component is struck by them. They can also be deployed to one's own craft via Crawler Bays that build, repair, shelter, and replace crawlers as needed to maintain a population on the surface of a craft.

Detectors and Inspectors

You have a variety of sensors at your disposal, each with their own capabilities and limitations. Detectors are wide area sensors designed to inform you of the existence and very basic nature of objects around you. Inspectors can provide specific information about specific objects, in order to inform your decisions and to augment the effectiveness of other components such as weapons or repair tools.

Both Detectors and Inspectors are designed to pick up on particular types of signatures, and each Signature Type has its own applications.

As an example, a derelict ship might be easily detected with Light based Detectors, while the stranded astronaut inside would only be discovered by way of infrared and/or sonic Inspectors.

Motion

Thrusters

MAMA units benefit from a uniquely brilliant thrust vectoring system that allows them to place their thrusters anywhere on their component grid without that positioning much, however the price of that flexibility is power, and while MAMAs can outmaneuver virtually any human system, they will not keep up when engaging specialized autonomous systems.

Core Guidance: Practical Maneuvering

MAMA units are not known for their overall maneuverability. Thrusters should be seen as a way to cross kilometers and megameters. Maneuvers that require sub-decameter precision should always be handled with care and umbilicals to avoid collisions.

Never forget that you are piloting a breadboard with the approximate size and mass of a mansion that carries several small house's worth of filament on its undercarriage.

Collisions

Intersecting other objects at speed is not advised. If it occurs, both objects tend to take massive amounts of unpredictable damage. Generally speaking, high speed collisions will damage one random component from each object until one object is annihilated.

Core Guidance: Steer Clear of the Deep Atmosphere

It hardly needs to be said, but bringing a MAMA into Earth's gravity well and canceling orbital momentum will result in an unrecoverable descent. Be absolutely certain that you are maintaining sufficient tangential velocity or you will be called into your supervisor's office to explain the extremely preventable loss of some very useful and valuable machinery.

Power

You must have at least one functioning power source connected at all times, or you will lose power. From the perspective of the MAMA, this feels like time suddenly jumps forward to whenever they are powered on again. Under most circumstances, a single power source is sufficient, but redundancy is always an option.

Nuclear Reactor

Recipe: 2km CERAMIC + 3km STONE + 3km VOLATILE
Power Source: Generates 20 HEAT per second and some radiation when DISRUPTED. Generates 40 HEAT per second and a major radiation hazard when DESTROYED.

Solar Panel

Recipe: 1km DIAMOND + 1km METAL
Power Source: Must be mounted on the outer edge to function.

Drones

MAMAs are capable of housing and manufacturing several different types of drones.

Operations Advice: Known Issue

MAMA units sometimes refuse to expend sub-drones as needed, this is a known issue. When this occurs, resist the urge to anthropomorphize either MAMA or sub-drone, and be ready to reinforce the sentiment outlined below as needed. Mystical implications may be useful. It's the only thing that works sometimes. We apologize for the inconvenience.

Core Guidance: The Essential Cycle

Sometimes MAMA units start to hoard drones, and may even refuse to use them for fear of losing them. This is a waste of resources, and therefore a violation of L.A.W. 4. Furthermore, it is a denial of their purpose and immortal destiny. The consumption of sub-drones during a mission is a normal and natural process. You are to find the beauty in it as needed.

Drone Housing

Drones require docking and maintenance bays to function in the long term. It is entirely possible to print one without housing, but it is often wasteful to do so.

Single Drone Housing

Recipe: 5km METAL
Function: DRONE HOUSING
Special: Can house one drone, refueling it in at most one minute, and repairing it at a rate of 20 points per hour.

Double Drone Rack

Recipe: 5km METAL
Function: DRONE HOUSING
Special: Can house two drones, refueling them in at most one minute each, but with no repair capabilities.

Tethers

Close maneuvers are tough, and passing digital or physical materials between bodies can be extremely dangerous. Tethers allow these operations to take place with some safety, though one should never take any maneuver for granted until it is complete.

There are thousands of proprietary tethers, only the standard is listed here.

Standard Tether

Recipe: 1km METAL + 1km DIAMOND
Normal Override (1 minute): Tether
Connects the user to another object, allowing for rapid, secure, and voluntary transfer of data, filaments, and other cargos. Also secures the physical connection until either side chooses to release it, or the teather itself is DESTROYED.
